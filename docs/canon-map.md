# Canon Map — Stunspot’s Guide to Macroeconomics

This canon maps macroeconomics as a layered operating system: monetary instruments sit inside balance sheets; balance sheets transmit liquidity; liquidity changes regimes; regimes shape policy, markets, trade, innovation, public reality, crisis dynamics, and institutional strategy.

The sequence matters. The early reports define the primitives—money, credit, collateral, liquidity, causality. The middle reports move into institutions and markets. The final reports translate macro structure into information dynamics, crisis diagnosis, and strategic action.

Source reports live in [`knowledge-packs/by-report/`](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/tree/main/knowledge-packs/by-report). `docs/` is only the navigation and guidance layer.

## Volume 1 — Foundations of Monetary Reality, Liquidity, and Economic Causality

**Use this volume to give a model the canon’s base ontology.** It defines what money is operationally, how liquidity moves through balance sheets and markets, and why macroeconomic change must be read through regimes rather than isolated indicators.

### [A. Monetary Ontology & Economic Reality Models — The Nature of Money, Credit, Collateral, and Financial Abstraction](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/a-monetary-ontology-and-economic-reality-models.md)

Report A starts with the apparent simplicity of money and dismantles it. It treats money as a hierarchy of settlement claims, credit promises, bank liabilities, central bank reserves, collateral instruments, and convertibility relationships. This report establishes the canon’s monetary vocabulary: money versus credit, inside versus outside money, reserves versus deposits, liquidity versus solvency, and the balance-sheet physics that become visible under stress.

### [B. Liquidity Physics and Global Capital Flow Dynamics — The Mechanics of Liquidity Creation, Destruction, and Transmission Across Financial Systems](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/b-liquidity-physics-and-global-capital-flow-dynamics.md)

Report B moves from monetary instruments to liquidity motion. It explains how funding conditions are created, transmitted, amplified, and destroyed across repo markets, bank balance sheets, offshore dollar systems, collateral networks, and global capital channels. This is the canon’s plumbing layer: the report that tells the model where macro-financial pressure actually travels.

### [C. Macro-Causal Architecture & Economic Regime Theory — A Systems-Level Framework for Economic Cycles, Regime Shifts, and Structural Transitions](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/c-macro-causal-architecture-and-economic-regime-theory.md)

Report C provides the causal grammar. It treats the macroeconomy as a complex, regime-dependent system where the same signal can imply different outcomes depending on leverage, policy stance, balance-sheet constraints, inflation structure, and institutional reaction functions. It is the bridge from static economics to macro systems analysis.

## Volume 2 — Monetary Governance, Institutional Control, and Policy Transmission

**Use this volume to explain who controls monetary and fiscal conditions, by what mechanisms, and under which constraints.** It covers the institutional machinery that shapes credit, debt, liquidity, and policy response.

### [D. Central Banking Systems & Monetary Intervention Architecture — Institutional Monetary Control, Policy Instruments, and Liquidity Management Systems](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/d-central-banking-systems-and-monetary-intervention-architecture.md)

Report D examines central banks as operational institutions rather than abstract rate-setting symbols. It maps policy instruments, reserve systems, lender/dealer-of-last-resort functions, balance-sheet operations, transmission channels, and the constraints that determine whether monetary intervention stabilizes or distorts the system.

### [E. Fiscal States, Sovereign Debt, and Political Capital Allocation — Government Spending Systems, Debt Structures, and Fiscal Constraint Dynamics](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/e-fiscal-states-sovereign-debt-and-political-capital-allocation.md)

Report E shifts to the state balance sheet. It frames fiscal policy as political capital allocation mediated by taxation, borrowing, sovereign credibility, debt markets, institutional capacity, and real resource constraints. This report is where the canon distinguishes sovereign monetary capacity from the actual limits of inflation, legitimacy, productive capacity, and funding structure.

### [F. Banking Systems, Credit Allocation, and Financial Intermediation — The Operational Architecture of Credit Creation and Capital Distribution](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/f-banking-systems-credit-allocation-and-financial-intermediation.md)

Report F covers the private credit engine. It explains how banks and financial intermediaries create deposits, allocate credit, manage capital, price risk, transmit policy, and amplify macro-financial cycles. It is the operational companion to Reports D and E: central banks and states set conditions, but banking systems distribute those conditions into the real economy.

## Volume 3 — Market Structure, Asset Regimes, and Capital Coordination

**Use this volume to connect macro conditions to asset prices, global power, and speculative capital formation.** It shows how liquidity regimes become market behavior and institutional allocation.

### [G. Asset Pricing Regimes & Cross-Market Liquidity Transmission — How Liquidity Conditions Shape Equities, Bonds, Real Estate, Commodities, and Venture Markets](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/g-asset-pricing-regimes-and-cross-market-liquidity-transmission.md)

Report G translates liquidity into asset behavior. It covers how rate structures, credit conditions, risk appetite, collateral values, duration exposure, and cross-market liquidity channels shape equities, bonds, real estate, commodities, and venture markets across regimes.

### [H. Global Trade Systems, Currency Hierarchies, and Geoeconomic Power](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/h-global-trade-systems-currency-hierarchies-and-geoeconomic-power.md)

Report H places macro-finance inside the global order. It examines trade structures, currency hierarchy, reserve status, external constraints, geoeconomic leverage, dollar dominance, and the institutional power embedded in payment systems and capital flows.

### [I. Innovation Finance, Venture Liquidity, and Speculative Capital Formation — The Macro Conditions Governing Innovation Funding and Technological Expansion](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/i-innovation-finance-venture-liquidity-and-speculative-capital-formation.md)

Report I treats innovation finance as a macro-liquidity phenomenon. Venture capital, speculative technology narratives, startup funding cycles, and long-duration innovation bets are not isolated from monetary conditions; they are strongly shaped by discount rates, capital abundance, liquidity preference, institutional incentives, and narrative coordination.

## Volume 4 — Information Systems, Strategic Signaling, and Narrative Liquidity

**Use this volume when the task requires interpretation, diagnostics, crisis awareness, or action.** It moves from macro structure to public reality, systemic breakdown, signal extraction, and institutional strategy.

### [J. Information Liquidity, Narrative Markets, and Attention Allocation Systems — How Financial Conditions Shape Visibility, Media Incentives, and Public-Reality Formation](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/j-information-liquidity-narrative-markets-and-attention-allocation-systems.md)

Report J introduces information liquidity: the idea that facts, narratives, warnings, and public beliefs have circulation constraints just as financial assets do. It examines how funding, distribution access, institutional endorsement, reputational safety, platform fit, and market actionability determine what becomes publicly real.

### [K. Financial Instability, Crisis Cascades, and Systemic Collapse Mechanics — Failure Propagation, Contagion Dynamics, and Macro-System Breakdown Patterns](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/k-financial-instability-crisis-cascades-and-systemic-collapse-mechanics.md)

Report K is the failure map. It covers systemic instability, contagion, forced deleveraging, funding runs, collateral spirals, confidence breaks, crisis cascades, and the mechanics by which local fragilities become macro-system breakdowns.

### [L. Macro Diagnostics, Regime Detection, and Economic Intelligence Systems — Operational Frameworks for Interpreting Macro Signals and Detecting Structural Transitions](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/l-macro-diagnostics-regime-detection-and-economic-intelligence-systems.md)

Report L is the diagnostic toolkit. It treats macro intelligence as regime-aware signal interpretation rather than indicator watching. It covers dynamic factor models, regime-switching frameworks, global liquidity indicators, offshore funding signals, systemic liquidity grids, early-warning structures, and practical economic intelligence systems.

### [M. Institutional Macro Strategy, Adaptive Positioning, and Strategic Coordination — Applying Macro Intelligence to Capital Allocation, Organizational Survival, and Strategic Action](https://github.com/Stunspot/stunspots-guide-to-macroeconomics/blob/main/knowledge-packs/by-report/m-institutional-macro-strategy-adaptive-positioning-and-strategic-coordination.md)

Report M translates the canon into action. It explains how institutions convert macro diagnosis into exposure mapping, liquidity capacity, optionality, hedging design, timing discipline, governance coordination, narrative credibility, and after-action review. Its governing concern is preserving organizational choice before hostile regimes compress decision space.
