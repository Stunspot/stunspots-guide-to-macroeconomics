# Stunspot’s Guide to Macroeconomics

**A model-facing macroeconomics canon for monetary systems, liquidity regimes, financial instability, and institutional strategy.**

This site is the navigation layer for *Stunspot’s Guide to Macroeconomics*. The actual corpus lives in the repository’s `knowledge-packs/` directories, where it can be loaded into AI workspaces, RAG systems, long-context sessions, agent memory layers, project knowledge bases, and local research environments.

The guide is human-readable, but its primary operational audience is the model. It is designed to provide structured macro-financial doctrine: monetary hierarchy, liquidity mechanics, credit creation, policy transmission, fiscal constraint, asset regimes, global currency hierarchy, narrative liquidity, systemic crisis mechanics, diagnostic frameworks, and institutional strategy.

| Omnibus | `knowledge-packs/omnibus/` | 1 whole-corpus bundle for large-context or archival use. |

## Canon Premise

Macroeconomics is treated here as a layered operating system: money, credit, collateral, liquidity, institutions, balance sheets, expectations, policy constraints, and narrative coordination interacting under changing regimes.

The canon is meant to help models avoid flat indicator commentary. A good answer should distinguish the active regime, identify the relevant transmission channels, preserve source traceability, and translate macro signals into decision-relevant reasoning without pretending that static facts settle dynamic conditions.

## Recommended First Use

For most AI/RAG workflows, start with the four files in `knowledge-packs/compiled-packs/`. They preserve the canon’s sequence while keeping file count manageable.

Use `knowledge-packs/by-report/` when you need precise report-level citation or selective ingestion. Use `knowledge-packs/omnibus/` when your system handles large single-file knowledge sources well.
